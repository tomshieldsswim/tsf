Our Mission

The Mission

Too many young athletes are carrying it silently, alone, with no framework for what they're feeling and no permission to ask for help. We meet them where they are.

"No intake form catches the kid who's learned to perform fine. No waiting room reaches the one who'd never walk through the door. The people in the room every day are set up to miss it too — until someone gives them the tools to see it."
, Tom Shields, Founder

Tom's Story

He Nearly Didn't Make It

Tom Shields is a two-time Olympic gold medalist. He is also someone who, in late 2018, experienced a serious mental health crisis after years of silent struggle.

He came through it because his wife Gianna turned around on her commute and called. That phone call changed everything.

Tom went public in December 2019, not because it was easy, but because he believed silence was costing lives. He returned to the Olympics. Today he is one of the only elite athletes in the world to have spoken publicly about a mental health crisis and come back.

What We Do

How We Help

Funded by the swim community. Delivered where athletes already are.

01
Mental Health Appointments

TSF funds subsidized appointments with licensed mental health professionals for youth athletes who need support and don't know where to start.

02
Mental Health First Aid

We install MHFA certification at swim clubs, training coaches and staff to recognize the signs of mental health crisis and respond with confidence.

03
Powered by Swim-a-Thons

Tom Shields Aquatics runs Swim-a-Thons at clubs nationwide. Tom and participating clubs donate a portion of proceeds directly to TSF to fund programming.

04
Community Funded

The foundation is built to be self-sustaining through the swim community, keeping the mission focused and the funding close to the athletes it serves.
