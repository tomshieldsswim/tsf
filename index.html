The Mission

Too many young athletes are carrying what Tom carried — silently, alone, with no framework for what they're feeling and no permission to ask for help. The Tom Shields Foundation delivers mental health education workshops for youth athletes, funded by our community swim events and swim-a-thons.

"The conversation that saves a life doesn't always happen in a therapist's office. Sometimes it happens in a locker room, after a talk from someone who's been there."
— Tom Shields, Founder

Tom's Story

He Nearly Didn't Make It

Tom Shields is one of the most decorated butterfly swimmers in American history. He is also someone who, in late 2018, hit his lowest point after years of silent struggle with depression — and attempted suicide.

He survived because his wife Gianna turned around on her commute home after something told her to call. That phone call saved his life.

Tom went public with his story in December 2019 — not because it was easy, but because he believed silence was costing lives. He returned to compete at the Olympic level. Today, he's one of the only elite athletes in the world to have spoken publicly about a suicide attempt and come back.

What We Do

The Workshop

01
Tom's Story

An honest, unfiltered account of what it looks like to struggle under elite athletic pressure — and what recovery actually looks like.

02
Psychological Tools

Practical, evidence-informed techniques for managing stress, regulating anxiety, processing failure, and building emotional resilience.

03
Whole-Person Framework

A direct challenge to "push through it" culture — replacing it with the understanding that caring for your mind is part of being a great athlete.

04
Open Conversation

A structured space where young athletes can ask real questions, share what they're carrying, and realize they are not alone.
