Why It Matters

The Problem We're Solving

Youth athletes face a unique and under-addressed mental health crisis. The pressure of performance, the fear of failure, and an athletic culture that equates struggle with weakness — all while carrying the same burdens every young person carries.

Most will never see a therapist. But they will show up to practice. We meet them there.

The Program

The Workshop

Each workshop is led by Tom Shields and trained facilitators. It's 60–90 minutes, delivered in person at schools, swim clubs, and sports organizations. No pool required.

01
Tom's Story

An honest, unfiltered account of what it looks like to struggle under elite athletic pressure — and what recovery actually looks like.

02
Psychological Tools

Practical, evidence-informed techniques for managing stress, regulating anxiety, processing failure, and building emotional resilience.

03
Whole-Person Framework

A direct challenge to "push through it" culture — replacing it with the understanding that caring for your mind is part of being a great athlete.

04
Open Conversation

A structured space where young athletes can ask real questions, share what they're carrying, and realize they are not alone.

How It Works

Built to Be Self-Sustaining

The Foundation is designed to fund itself through community events — so we're never dependent on grants or major donors to deliver the mission.

1

Swim Events

Community swim-a-thons and charity meets raise funds through pledges, entry fees, and sponsorships.

2

Revenue

Proceeds cover operations, talent, and curriculum — and build toward a long-term endowment.

3

Workshops

Funds are used to deliver free or subsidized mental health workshops to youth athletes.

4

Scholarships

Surplus flows into scholarships for athletes and communities who can't afford programming.
